I am a PhD student in the Theory Group at Columbia University, co-advised by Tal Malkin and Henry Yuen. My research focuses on cryptography and quantum computing, and more broadly on computational complexity.

In 2024 I graduated from the University of Texas at Austin with B.S. degrees in Computer Science (Honors) and Mathematics and a minor in Chinese language. I was named a Dean’s Honored Graduate in the College of Natural Sciences with a distinction in research and received an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship. Previously I worked as an undergraduate researcher with David Soloveichik on theoretical aspects of molecular and unconventional computing.

I enjoy learning languages, solving crossword puzzles, playing board games, and running.

I am happy to chat about theoretical computer science and related topics—feel free to reach out.
